NEW! Mechatronics Industrial Automation Program

This program prepares students for high-demand jobs in the Central Valley, such as field service technician and maintenance mechanic.

Program Overview

Gain hands-on, practical experience in welding, hydraulics, electricity, mechanical fundamentals, and machine alignment!

Build skills in electronics, computerized equipment maintenance, and preventative/predictive maintenance.

Learn and apply predictive and preventative maintenance methods; the basics of analysis, vibration analysis, and thermal analysis applications; and use and apply a computerized maintenance management system.

Earn a certificate or an Associate degree.

The program prepares students for the National Institute of Metal Working Skills (NIMS) Industrial Technician Maintenance (ITM) credentialing program.

Spring 2025 Classes

  • IT 10: Maintenance Operations (3 Units)

    • Covers interpreting maintenance documentation, performing predictive and preventative maintenance, reading technical drawings, and safely using hand tools. Students will also practice troubleshooting techniques and will receive an OSHA 10-hour course completion card upon successful completion.

  • IT 11: Basic Mechanical Systems (3 Units)

    • Teaches students to follow mechanical power transmission safety rules and covers installation, alignment, lubrication, and troubleshooting of systems involving chains, belts, bearings, and gear trains. Dimensional measuring tools will be used in practical applications.

  • IT 20: Basic Hydraulic Systems (2 Units)

    • Covers hydraulic system principles, including fluid power schematics, flow and pressure characteristics, hydraulic system components, and troubleshooting, with an emphasis on fluid power safety regulations.

  • IT 21: Basic Pneumatic Systems (2 Units)

    • Explores the principles of pneumatics using air compressors, air treatment, valves, logic devices, and cylinders. Fluid power safety regulations are emphasized.

  • IT 30: Electrical Systems (4 Units)

    • Introduces the theory of electricity, covering voltage, current, resistance, and power in AC and DC circuits. Students will learn to analyze circuit operations, understand motor control symbols, and explore wiring diagrams and components like relays, switches, and sensing devices.
